Model Evaluation Overview

To ensure the Tibetan OCR model delivers accurate and meaningful text recognition, we evaluate its performance using two key metrics: Character Error Rate (CER) and Word Error Rate (WER).

Character Error Rate (CER)

CER measures how accurately the model recognizes individual Tibetan characters. It calculates the number of character insertions, deletions, or substitutions required to match the models output with the correct text. A lower CER indicates higher accuracy at the character level, which is essential for Tibetan, where small character errors can significantly change meaning.

Word Error Rate (WER)

WER evaluates the accuracy of full word recognition. It counts how many words were inserted, deleted, or substituted compared to the ground truth. A low WER means the model is doing well at understanding complete words, making the recognized text more readable and reliable.
