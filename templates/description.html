Tibetan OCR Model Prediction

Overview

OCR (Optical Character Recognition) is a technology used to convert images or scanned documents into editable and searchable text. Tibetan language OCR is more complex due to the script’s structure, vertical stacking, and limited training data.

Tibetan Script Characteristics

  • Written from left to right using the Uchen script.
  • Characters are often stacked vertically within syllables.
  • Involves complex ligatures and diacritics.
  • There is a lack of large, labeled datasets for training.

Objective

The objective of a Tibetan OCR (Optical Character Recognition) model prediction system is to accurately recognize and convert printed or handwritten Tibetan text from scanned images or photographs into machine-readable, editable, and searchable digital text.

Specific Objectives:

  • Digitization of Tibetan scriptures, books, and historical documents.
  • Preservation of Tibetan language and culture through digital archiving.
  • Providing accessibility for researchers, educators, and students.
  • Automating manual transcription of Tibetan texts.
  • Enabling downstream applications such as translation, text-to-speech, and linguistic analysis.

Who Benefits?

  • Researchers: Transparent, quantitative model comparisons.
  • Scholars & Lamas: Insights into which models best preserve spiritual nuance.
  • Developers: Guidance on which APIs deliver top-quality translations for applications.
